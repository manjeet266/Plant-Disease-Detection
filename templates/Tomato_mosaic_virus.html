The virus may be introduced on infected seed. Only a small number of seedlings need to be infected for the virus to spread rapidly. It can also be spread on contaminated tools and the clothing and hands of workers during routine activities.


Solution for Disease

Proper and timely care is important for pest and disease control in tomatoes. This not only prevents the spread of the disease but also promotes the growth of the crop and produces immune plants throughout its life cycle. In this way the crops meet their expectations and produce a vigorous, healthy and succulent crop.


-Create a healthy nursery
-Use varieties with immune system
-Protect the nursery already from carrier insects
-Increase plant immunity by proper nutrition and water management
-See that there will be sterile planting. Avoid spreading the disease by removing infected seedlings
